copy, redistribute, translate, crop, annotate, and adapt its covered material,
including for teaching and commercial work, without asking for separate
permission.

## How to give credit

Keep the credit close to the reused material where practical. Include the
creator, manual title, precise source, licence, and a change note. Recording the
manual version matters because stable and nightly pages can differ.

Use this pattern:

> Rensu P. Theart, *VIPP User Manual*, [stable version or nightly commit],
> [title and URL of the source page], licensed under [CC BY
> 4.0](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/4.0/). Changes: [none, or a
> concise description].

For example:

> Adapted from Rensu P. Theart, *VIPP User Manual*, version 0.11.0a1,
> “Build your first workflow,” [source page URL], licensed under CC BY 4.0.
> Changes: screenshot cropped and labels added.

An exact page link is preferable to the site home page. If space is limited,
you may put the full attribution in a figure caption, credits page, slide
notes, or another location that readers can readily find.

Repository configuration, automation, templates, and custom styles are also
outside this documentation-specific CC BY 4.0 grant unless a file states
otherwise.

Screenshots can contain third-party interface elements, names, trademarks, or
data. Those components retain their existing rights. The documentation licence
covers only the original screenshot authorship that the copyright holder can
license and does not imply endorsement by napari or another third party.

## Attribution is not a scientific citation

Licence attribution satisfies a reuse condition; it does not replace a
scholarly citation when VIPP contributed to research. Follow the project's
current citation guidance as well as crediting any manual material you reuse.
